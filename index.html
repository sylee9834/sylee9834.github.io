Hi there, I am Seung Yeon Lee.

My research focuses on empirical and quantitative approaches to marketing questions related to AI, digital platforms, the entertainment industry and social media.

I am particularly interested in how consumers respond to emerging technologies, policy changes, and broader societal changes, as well as how firms can respond to and shape these effects through marketing strategy and communication.

More broadly, my work combines marketing theory with large-scale digital trace data, text analysis, machine learning, and causal inference methods to understand consumer behavior and firm strategy in dynamic markets.

Publications

The Impact of Co-Production Partnerships on Film Success: The Moderating Role of Participating Countries and Cultural Differences

Journal of the Korea Entertainment Industry Association, 19(1), 1-12, 2025

with Chaehyeon Lee and Sue Ryung Chang

See more
  • Analyzes a dataset of 6,724 films released worldwide to examine how co-production partnerships affect box office performance.
  • Finds that the number of participating production companies positively affects box office performance, while the number of participating countries and cultural differences weaken this effect.

The Impact of Positive Emotional Valence in Webnovel Blurbs on Its Success

Journal of the Korea Contents Association, 24(3), 206-217, 2024

with Chaehyeon Lee and Sue Ryung Chang

See more
  • Constructed a dataset of 4,637 webnovels via web crawling and fine-tuned a pretrained Korean BERT model for sentiment analysis on webnovel blurbs.
  • Estimated Tobit regression models showing that positive emotional valence in blurbs increases viewership, with effects moderated by blurb length and author popularity.
